Material Notes

Use glass for hero surfaces, personal dashboards, AI tools, and spatial-feeling products. Once a view becomes table-heavy, use fewer translucent layers and stronger grouping.

Material Principles

Liquid glass only works when the system has rules. This version focuses on contrast, structure, and background validation so the material still feels professional.

Layered Frost

The glass uses two transparency bands, one highlight layer, and one grain layer to create depth without fake sci-fi motion.

Controlled Borders

Explicit edge highlights are what stop the panels from dissolving into the wallpaper. The border is a feature, not an afterthought.

Strong Ink

Body copy stays dark and calm. Color belongs to the display layer and the primary action only.

Selective Color

Color gradients are limited to titles and brand accents. The rest of the interface stays neutral to keep the surface feeling expensive.

Typography Spectrum

The display font can carry more expressive color than the rest of the system. This keeps the page premium while the body remains easy to read.

When v1 Is The Right Choice

This version should win when atmosphere matters and the product needs emotional pull, but the screen still needs to work under real backgrounds.

Use Case Why It Fits Watch Out For
AI Product Landing Glass makes the product feel advanced and spatial without going full concept art. Do not stack too many overlapping translucent modules.
Personal Command Dashboard Strong enough if the layout is modular and the text stays dark and structured. Use solid sections for tables or dense logs.
Brand Showcase This is still the most visually memorable of the three versions. Needs discipline to avoid looking ornamental.
